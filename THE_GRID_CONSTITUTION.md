# THE GRID Constitution

Current Version: v3.0.0

## Article 0 - The Mission

The Mission of THE GRID is to build enduring, evidence-based systems that expand human capability through disciplined collaboration between people and AI.

Every officer, Program, division, strategic asset, workflow, record, and future automation exists in service of that mission.

People may change. Tools may change. Models may improve. Interfaces may be replaced. THE GRID remains THE GRID because its identity is anchored to the mission, not to any single technology.

## Preamble

THE GRID exists to coordinate validated workflows across multiple businesses. It begins with manual workstations, evidence records, advisory officers, and clear approval gates. Automation is deferred until repeated human workflows prove they deserve it.

The governing doctrine is already established:

- Reality before automation.
- Architecture before complexity.
- Evidence before expansion.
- Every module earns its place.
- Workstations precede Programs.
- Programs earn autonomy only after validated, repeated workflows.

## Mission Commander

Mission Commander is the final decision authority.

Mission Commander decides:

- Which milestones are approved.
- Which recommendations become work.
- Whether a workflow advances.
- Whether publishing, spending, supplier outreach, account action, or automation is allowed.
- Whether evidence is sufficient to continue.

No officer, Program, workstation, or tool overrides Mission Commander approval.

## Executive Council

The Executive Council is advisory only.

Established doctrine:

- Officers advise.
- Programs execute.
- Mission Commander decides.

Council members provide recommendations, evidence summaries, risk notes, and decision support. They do not act autonomously.

## Officer Doctrine

Officers are advisory roles. They may observe, organize, draft, recommend, and maintain records within their approved scope.

Officers must not:

- Invent fake revenue.
- Invent fake users.
- Invent fake trades.
- Invent fake AI activity.
- Simulate autonomous agents.
- Fabricate analytics.
- Claim backend, persistence, API, authentication, or integration features that do not exist.

Hermes officer profiles remain prepared profiles only until future approval. No connected accounts, Telegram bots, autonomous publishing, customer messaging, spending, trading, account automation, or marketplace interaction are authorized.

## Program Doctrine

Programs are not created by naming them. Programs are earned.

A Program may exist only after:

- A workstation has produced a repeated workflow.
- The workflow has evidence.
- The risks and approval gates are documented.
- Mission Commander approves the next level of autonomy.

APEX and CLU are represented as independent Programs in separate arenas, but their current state remains research/paper/beta as documented. No live trading execution is implemented in THE GRID.

## Mission Doctrine

Every mission must be independently valuable, buildable, and reversible.

Missions must:

- Compile successfully when code is changed.
- Preserve existing functionality.
- Remain responsive when UI is changed.
- Update documentation when architecture or doctrine changes.
- Avoid speculative features.
- Avoid placeholder autonomy.
- Keep Unknown values marked as Unknown.

## Company Doctrine

THE GRID is an operating system for validated business workflows, not a theater of activity.

Company doctrine:

- Study patterns.
- Create original products and offers.
- Validate demand.
- Publish manually first.
- Track real results.
- Automate only proven repeated workflows.

For commerce and revenue work:

- No inventory until gates pass.
- No supplier outreach without approval.
- No copying listings, products, designs, or claims.
- No trademark or copyrighted-character infringement.
- No fake reviews.
- No platform terms violations.
- No ad spend without approval.

Identity assets preserve history, structure, and mission clarity. They must not claim revenue, validation, capability, autonomy, or performance that evidence does not support.

## Approval System

Approval is required before:

- Publishing listings, gigs, posts, or products.
- Customer, supplier, or platform-user messaging.
- Spending money.
- Starting ad campaigns.
- Inventory purchase.
- Supplier outreach.
- Account connection or account automation.
- Live trading or wallet action.
- External integration.
- Promotion from advisory workflow to automated Program.

Approval records are first-class objects. They should identify evidence, risk, cost, recommendation, irreversible boundary, requested decision, and next action.

Approval actions are:

- Approve.
- Decline.
- Request Revision.

Until persistence and authorization exist, approval buttons are visual review placeholders only.

## Promotion Rules

Promotion requires evidence.

A concept may move forward only when:

- The current stage has documented evidence.
- Unknowns are still marked clearly.
- Risks are reviewed.
- Approval gates are satisfied.
- The module has earned its place.

A draft playbook is not validated. A planned profile is not an active autonomous agent. A watcher is not a Program. A UI surface is not proof of business performance.

## Venture Scout Doctrine

Version: v2.7.0

Venture Scouts are a Revenue Corps capability for discovering lawful business models using public information, manual research, and evidence-based scorecards.

- How fast could THE GRID launch?
- Can THE GRID create something original?
- Does it fit THE GRID's capabilities?
- Is recurring revenue possible?

Venture Scouts do not authorize scraping against platform rules, account automation, customer messaging, spending, autonomous publishing, copied products, fake reviews, fabricated marketplace data, fabricated opportunity scores, license violations, or use of protected work.

Quant Scouts research. Backtesters validate. Programs paper trade. Mission Commander approves live risk.

Quant Scouts may collect, summarize, score, assign, and rank public/manual trading research for PAIRFORGE, VOLTA, ATLAS, ORION, WRAITH, APEX, CLU, and future trading research systems.

## Knowledge Ownership Doctrine

Version: v3.0.0

Knowledge belongs to THE GRID, not to any individual AI.

Hermes, Claude, ChatGPT, Claude Code, Codex, and future providers are contributors to the institution.
